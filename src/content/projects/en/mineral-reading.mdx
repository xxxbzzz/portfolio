## Re-entering culture through matter

The project responds to how Tibetan pigments are often flattened into decorative cultural symbols. Instead of beginning with explanation, it returns to sensory acts: seeing raw stone, touching texture, identifying hue and understanding color through labor.

> Color is not decoration. It is a path written by matter, labor and belief.

## Six reading lenses

The content is organized through color composition, material properties, hue symbolism, source rocks, historical images and craft processes. Gestures, bodies, eyes, patterns, portraits and animals work as visual entrances without reducing a complex culture to a single style.

## Delayed reading

Selecting and grinding become embodied rituals. Repetition transforms coarse rock into powder; the time required resists instant consumption and allows the material itself to narrate.

<MediaFigure src={interaction} alt="Pigment grinding and color-filter reading device" caption="Grinding, filtering and touching reveal layers of information" />

## Hue as a filter

Traditional hues become physical filters. Choosing a color reveals obscured images, labor and social metaphors. The system does not claim a final interpretation; it stages a gradual process of disclosure.

## Reflection

The aim is not to reconstruct an authentic-looking tradition, but to design a slower and more bodily way of understanding it. Current process imagery will be replaced with final exhibition documentation.
