## Observation

Comments, danmaku and tags no longer remain peripheral. Popular reactions frame how a text is encountered and can even replace the original as the main source of interpretation. We call these attached yet influential layers digital paratext.

> What happens when the physical positions of primary text and paratext are forcibly exchanged?

## Interface is power

Primary content above and comments below is not a neutral convention. It quietly decides who occupies the center and who may only respond. Reversing that order creates cognitive friction and exposes the interface as an editor of meaning.

## A fictional press

VICE-VOICE Press follows one position: publishing is not the end, but the beginning of discussion. A book is treated as an open field shaped by authors, readers, commenters and platforms.

## Between the lines and outside them

One volume inserts danmaku through the primary text. A second set gathers comments into five emotional modes: echo, call, confession, dialogue and cry. Paper, typography, spacing and color encode emotional distance and interaction data.

## From book to platform

A prototype generates paratext around any keyword. A parallel online experiment serializes serious writing inside social-media comment threads, using the platform itself as a networked book.

<MediaFigure src={prototype} alt="Digital paratext prototype" caption="Prototype and parasitic publishing platform" temporary />

## Exhibition

The installation brings together the books, comment volumes, prototype and exhibition graphics. Visitors can observe how paratext directs interpretation and leave attached texts of their own.

<MediaFigure src={exhibition} alt="VICE-VOICE exhibition display" caption="Physical publications and exhibition display" wide />

## Reflection

The project does not claim that comments are more truthful than primary text. It makes the hidden hierarchy visible and shows that reading is choreographed by layouts, metrics and platform rules as much as by words.
