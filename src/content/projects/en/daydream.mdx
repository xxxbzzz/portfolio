## When the everyday bends

Daydream begins with an ordinary walk through campus and nearby streets. Live footage stays monochrome while drawn lines, blocks and figures briefly escape attention and build another logic on top of reality.

<VideoPoster poster={cover} url="https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1rz421k7SY/" label="Play Daydream on Bilibili" />

## Between footage and drawing

The live footage provides a movement scaffold. Saturated blue, orange and pink marks are then drawn frame by frame. Rather than explaining the scene, they expose associations, substitutions and visual mistakes produced while walking.

## Sound and rhythm

Field recordings and electronic sounds trigger drawn movements. The edit repeatedly drifts away and returns, making the viewing experience feel like entering someone else's stream of attention.

## Reflection

This study was my first attempt to use drawing as a time-based medium. It also became a starting point for later spatial and interactive work: how can invisible mental activity leave traces on the surface of reality?
